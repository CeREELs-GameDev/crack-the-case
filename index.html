"I see you." Mama smiled. "And I see Frannie Jo sleeping right behind me." She glanced up in the rearview mirror at my little sister, who was snuggled up with our dog, Biscuit. Both of them were snoring sweetly, cuddled against all the clothes that we'd piled in the way-back seat. Frannie's nearly six, but people think she's even younger than that because she's so small. She blended in easy with the books and blankets and clothes. "I got all the magic I ever need here with me," Mama sighed. I smiled at her words. I wanted them to be true, but I knew Frannie and I didn't have the kind of magic necessary to get rid of Mama's sad. But maybe that kind of magic did exist somewhere.
